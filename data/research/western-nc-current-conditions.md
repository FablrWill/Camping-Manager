# Western NC Current Conditions

> **As of:** April 2, 2026
> **Note:** This document covers perishable, time-sensitive conditions. Always verify against current USFS alerts and NCFS status before a trip.

---

## 🔥 Burn Ban — ACTIVE STATEWIDE

**NC Forest Service statewide open burning ban effective March 28, 2026.**

- Applies to: campfires, fire pit fires, yard waste burns, debris burns, land-clearing burns
- Exception: contained BBQ grills for outdoor food preparation only
- Enforced by Buncombe County Fire Marshal and NC Forest Service
- Urban note: open burning is never permitted within Asheville city limits regardless of ban status
- No timeline given for lift; expect it to remain through dry conditions
- **Bottom line for camping: no campfires. Bring a stove.**

## 🌫️ Air Quality

Code YELLOW as of April 1, 2026 for Asheville/Buncombe County valleys.

- Fine particulate matter: moderate risk
- Sensitive groups (elderly, children, respiratory/cardiovascular conditions) should limit prolonged outdoor exertion
- Smoke from Jumping Branch fire specifically impacting Buck Creek and Marion corridors

## 🌦️ Weather & Drought (April 2026)

- Temperatures running above historical average for April
- Precipitation well below normal — prolonged drought conditions
- Increased fuel load from Helene downed timber creates severe fire danger
- Long-range: summer forecast for cooler-than-normal temps in the north, below-normal rainfall

**Camping impacts:**
- Water sources at higher elevations less reliable; check specific source locations before relying on them
- Linville River main channel already contaminated (see linville-gorge-intel.md); drought doesn't improve that
- No campfires even if burn ban lifts; conditions warrant continued caution

## 🌲 Pisgah Ranger District — Roadside Camping Rule (Ongoing)

**1,000-foot road buffer in effect through September 1, 2026.**
Camping prohibited within 1,000 feet of any road open to public vehicle travel in Buncombe, Haywood, Henderson, and Transylvania counties — unless at a designated site shown on the district map.

This is not a temporary emergency order — it's the standing management framework for the Pisgah Ranger District. Affects Shining Rock / Black Balsam approach.
